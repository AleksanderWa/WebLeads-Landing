# Best Google Maps Scraper Tools in 2026

Google Maps has over 200 million business listings. For anyone doing local outreach, that is the most complete database of small and medium businesses available anywhere.

The problem is getting the data out in a useful form.

Copy-pasting from Google Maps takes hours. DIY scripts break when Google changes its layout. Most generic web scrapers return incomplete data and no email addresses.

This guide compares the tools people actually use to scrape Google Maps for leads in 2026. We cover what data each tool returns, how fresh it is, what the pricing looks like, and who each tool is right for.

---

## What to Look for in a Google Maps Scraper

Not all scrapers are created equal. Before picking one, check these five things:

**1. Data freshness.** Does the tool query Google Maps live, or pull from a cached database? Cached data is faster but can be months old. For local businesses that change frequently, freshness matters.

**2. Email extraction.** Google Maps does not show emails. To get them, the tool needs to crawl business websites. Many scrapers skip this step entirely and return just the Google listing data.

**3. Decision maker data.** Knowing the business name is not enough for outreach. You need to know who to contact. Some tools identify the owner, founder, or relevant decision maker by name and role.

**4. Ease of use.** API-based tools are flexible but require technical setup. If you want a list in 10 minutes without writing code, choose a tool built for non-technical users.

**5. Pricing model.** Some tools charge by result (credits per row). Others charge a flat monthly fee. At scale, credit-based pricing can get expensive fast.

### 2. Outscraper

Outscraper is a cloud-based API service for scraping Google Maps at scale. It returns standard Google Maps listing data and supports bulk searches via CSV upload.

**What you get back:** Business name, address, phone, website, coordinates, category, rating, review count. No email extraction. No decision maker data.

**What it costs:** Credit-based pricing. Roughly $3-5 per 1,000 results depending on volume. Free tier is minimal.

**Pros:**
- Good for bulk scraping at high volume
- API access for technical users
- Reliable uptime and support

**Cons:**
- Returns raw listing data only
- Emails and decision makers require additional tools
- Credits add up quickly at scale

**Best for:** Developers building data pipelines who want raw Maps data via API.

---

### 3. Apify (Google Maps Scraper Actor)

Apify is a cloud automation platform with a marketplace of pre-built "actors." The Google Maps Scraper actor extracts listing data from Google Maps on demand.

**What you get back:** Business name, address, phone, website, reviews, GPS coordinates, business hours. Email extraction is limited and depends on actor configuration.

**What it costs:** Apify charges by compute units. A free tier with $5 of monthly credits is available. Paid plans start at $49/month.

**Pros:**
- Flexible and configurable
- Good documentation
- Works well for technical users comfortable with JSON output

**Cons:**
- Setup requires comfort with API concepts
- Email extraction is inconsistent
- No decision maker data built in
- Pricing unpredictable for non-technical users

**Best for:** Technical users who want control over the scraping logic and are comfortable with API-based tools.

### 4. PhantomBuster

PhantomBuster is an automation tool primarily known for LinkedIn scraping. It also has a Google Maps Search Export phantom that extracts basic listing data.

**What you get back:** Business name, address, phone, website, rating. Very basic. Email extraction is not included.

**What it costs:** Starts at $56/month for 20 hours of phantom execution time.

**Pros:**
- Good if you already use PhantomBuster for LinkedIn work
- Simple interface

**Cons:**
- Google Maps is not PhantomBuster's main use case
- Limited data depth compared to purpose-built scrapers
- Relatively expensive for what you get from Maps
- Execution time limits affect large searches

**Best for:** Existing PhantomBuster users who want occasional Maps data without switching tools.

---

### 5. D7 Lead Finder

D7 Lead Finder is a lead database that pulls from Google Maps and other sources. It returns contact information including emails, which are sourced from its own database rather than live website crawling.

**What you get back:** Business name, address, phone, email (database), website, social links. Decision maker data is limited.

**What it costs:** Plans start around $33/month with credit limits on searches.

**Pros:**
- Emails included without separate crawling step
- Simple interface built for non-technical users
- Good for quick list building

**Cons:**
- Database-sourced emails may be outdated
- No live crawling means data freshness varies
- Decision maker information is thin

**Best for:** Users who want a simple tool with emails included and do not need the freshest possible data.

---

### 6. Manual Scraping (DataMiner, Copy-Paste)

DataMiner is a browser extension that lets you extract data from web pages by pointing and clicking on elements. You can use it on Google Maps to pull visible listing data into a spreadsheet.

Some people do this by hand, opening each listing, copying the details, and building a spreadsheet manually.

**What you get back:** Whatever is visible on the Google Maps page. Name, address, phone, website, rating. No emails. No decision maker data.

**What it costs:** DataMiner has a free tier with row limits. Paid plans from $19.99/month. Manual scraping costs only your time.

**Pros:**
- Low cost
- No account or API setup required

**Cons:**
- Extremely slow at any meaningful volume
- No email extraction
- Google anti-bot measures can interrupt browser-based tools
- Not scalable beyond a few dozen results

**Best for:** One-off small searches where you need a handful of results and do not want to sign up for a paid service.
