## Who Needs Mortgage Leads

This guide is for people selling services *to* mortgage professionals. Not for homeowners shopping for rates.

That includes:

- **Mortgage software vendors** (CRM, origination systems, compliance tools)
- **Real estate and mortgage education** (licensing, continuing education)
- **Marketing agencies** specializing in mortgage/real estate
- **Mortgage lead generation services** (selling leads to loan officers and brokers)
- **Third-party service providers** (appraisal management, title services, insurance)
- **Technology platforms** (document management, e-signature, compliance)
- **B2B services** (coaching, sales training, business development support)

All of these need access to active mortgage professionals who are making buying decisions.

## Why Mortgage Lists Don't Work

Mortgage is one of the fastest-changing industries. People move between companies constantly. Loan officers switch from banks to brokers. Brokers merge or go out of business. A "mortgage lender list" purchased today is already being outdated as you receive it.

## A Better Way: Search Google Maps for Mortgage Professionals

Here's what works: Google Maps is where mortgage companies actively manage their presence.

It's where customers leave reviews. It's where they post hours, contact info, and rates. It's where they demonstrate they're actively operating right now.

Google Maps data updates in real time. Companies that close get their listings inactive. New mortgage shops get listed within days. When a loan officer moves to a new broker, their new employer shows up in searches.

Instead of buying a stale list, search Google Maps. Here's how:

### 1. Decide Your Target Segment

Mortgage is broad. You could target:

- **Retail loan officers** (banks, credit unions, direct lenders)
- **Mortgage brokers** (independent, serve residential and commercial clients)
- **Commercial mortgage brokers** (business and investment property financing)
- **Mortgage wholesale lenders** (fund loans, don't originate directly)

Different segments have different needs. Target the right one.

### 2. Search Google Maps by Location and Type

Search for:
- "Mortgage lenders near [city]"
- "Mortgage brokers [metro area]"
- "Loan officers [neighborhood]"

You'll get every active mortgage company in that location.

### 3. Filter by Activity Level

Not all mortgage shops are equally active or successful. Filter by:

- **Google rating**: 4.5+ (below 4.0 = customer complaints or service issues)
- **Review count**: 50+ reviews = active loan origination, lots of customers
- **Review recency**: Recent reviews (last 2-4 weeks) = currently closing loans and building business

Narrow to mortgage professionals who are clearly thriving and actively originating loans.

### 4. Get Contact Information and Research

For each mortgage professional:
- **Website**: See their products, loan programs, and team size
- **Phone**: Quick qualification call if needed
- **Email**: Find via their website or LinkedIn
- **LinkedIn**: Understand their background and current role
- **Recent reviews**: Gauge customer satisfaction and deal volume

This approach gives you qualified mortgage professionals, not a bulk list of names.
