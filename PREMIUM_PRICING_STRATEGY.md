### 1.1 The Core Differentiator: Personal Emails Guaranteed

**What We Deliver**:
- ✅ john.smith@roofingco.com (personal, verified) - **PRIMARY CONTACTS**
- ✅ sarah.johnson@roofingco.com (personal, verified) - **PRIMARY CONTACTS**
- ✅ info@solocontractor.com (micro-business backup) - **BACKUP for 1-2 person businesses**

| Email Type | Example | Who Reads It | Response Rate | Personalization |
|------------|---------|--------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Personal** | john@company.com | Specific decision maker | 10-25% | "Hi John" ✓ |
| **Generic** | info@company.com | Unknown inbox | 1-5% | "Dear Sir/Madam" ✗ |

- C-level executives use personal emails ~50% of the time for business communications
- 73% of decision-makers expect personalization in B2B outreach

- Solo owners and 1-2 person operations often use generic emails (info@, contact@) as their primary business inbox

- Generic emails included ONLY for high-confidence micro-businesses (≤2 people, 0.80+ confidence)
- Labeled as "Micro-Business Backup" in exports so customers know the context
- Result: Complete coverage without compromising on quality

### 2.2 Deep Dive: Apollo.io

#### Apollo's Strengths
- Massive database: 275M contacts, 73M companies

- "Running out mid-campaign disrupts workflow"

- Focuses on LinkedIn professionals and enterprise companies
- Local SMBs (contractors, restaurants, retail) poorly represented
- Generic emails common for small businesses
- "Great for targeting C-suite executives, but for general contractors or small restaurants not on LinkedIn, Apollo doesn't help"

**3. Complexity Overkill**
- Built for enterprise SDR teams with dedicated sales ops
- Complex CRM integrations and workflows
- Steep learning curve for solo founders
- "Apollo should be evaluated as a usage-governed system... with actual spend driven by credit governance, enrichment strategy, AI feature adoption, and operational discipline"

**Translation**: Too complicated for SMBs who just want leads.

## 3. Unit Economics

### 3.1 Cost Structure

#### Variable Costs Per Delivered Lead

| Cost Component | Rate | Usage | Cost |
|----------------|------|-------|------|
| **Proxy bandwidth** | $1/GB | 0.6MB × 10 businesses | $0.006 |
| **SERP requests** | $1/1000 | 1 domain × 10 businesses | $0.010 |
| **Email verification** | $0.006/email | 1.8 emails × 10 businesses | $0.108 |
| **TOTAL** | | | **$0.124** |

**Key assumptions**:
- **10:1 scrape-to-lead ratio**: Must scrape 10 businesses to deliver 1 lead with verified personal email OR micro-business with generic email
- **1.8 emails verified**: Pattern learner optimizes to verify only high-confidence personal emails
- **Smart email filtering**: Personal emails prioritized; generic emails included only for detected micro-businesses (≤2 people)

#### Why 10:1 Conversion Rate?

```
100 businesses scraped from Maps
  ↓ (80% have websites)
80 businesses with websites
  ↓ (75% have emails extracted)
60 businesses with emails found
  ↓ (33% have personal emails discoverable)
20 businesses with personal emails
  ↓ (50% of personal emails verify successfully)
10 DELIVERED LEADS
```

**Conversion rate: 10%**

**Why only 33% have discoverable personal emails?**
- Many SMBs only list generic emails (info@, contact@)
- Personal emails require finding a person's name on the website
- Must construct or discover their email pattern
- Small businesses often don't have "Team" or "About" pages with names

**Micro-Business Coverage**:
- Businesses without personal emails undergo size estimation (review count + decision maker count)
- If classified as micro (1-2 people) with 0.80+ confidence, generic email included as backup
- Estimated 15-20% of leads will be micro-businesses with generic emails only
- This improves coverage without compromising response rates (owner checks the inbox)

## 5. Pattern Learner Strategy

### 5.1 Why Pattern Learner is CRITICAL

Without pattern learner, costs would be:
- **2x higher** verification spend (verify all emails vs selective)
- **40% lower margins** (45-50% vs 75-83%)
- **Unsustainable at scale**

With pattern learner:
- **37-43% cost reduction**
- **Learns which businesses have personal emails** before expensive extraction
- **Predicts best email patterns** to verify fewer emails
- **Improves over time** as more campaigns run

**This is not optional—it's core to unit economics.**

---

### 5.2 Three-Phase Learning Strategy

#### Phase 1: Discovery (First 20% of Campaign)

**Goal**: Learn patterns for this industry/location combination

**Process**:
```
For each business:
  1. Deep crawl website for names
     - About Us page
     - Team page
     - Contact page
     - Footer

  2. Extract ALL potential personal emails
     - Found emails (john@company.com)
     - Generated patterns (first.last@, flast@, first@)

  3. Verify ALL personal emails found (learning mode)

  4. Track success patterns:
     - Which page types have names? (Team: 85%, About: 65%, Contact: 15%)
```

### 6.4 Business Size Estimation

**Purpose**: Determine when to include generic emails as backup contacts for micro-businesses.

**Implementation**:
```python
class BusinessSizeEstimator:
    # Professions with high review-to-employee ratios
    HIGH_REVIEW_PROFESSIONS = {
        'doctor', 'dentist', 'lawyer', 'accountant', 'salon',
        'spa', 'therapist', 'optometrist', 'chiropractor', etc.
    }

    def estimate_size(place: Place) -> dict:
        """
        Estimate business size using:
        1. PRIMARY: Review count (profession-aware thresholds)
        2. VALIDATION: Validated decision maker count

        Returns: {
            'estimated_employees': int,
            'size_category': 'micro' | 'small' | 'medium',
            'confidence': float (0.0-1.0),
            'method': str
        }
        """

    def should_include_generic_email(place: Place) -> bool:
        """
        Include generic emails ONLY when:
        - size_category == 'micro' (1-2 people)
        - size_confidence >= 0.80 (high confidence)
        """
```

**Size Classification Logic**:

| Review Count | High-Review Profession | Normal Profession | Classification |
|--------------|------------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| 0-10 reviews | 1 person | 1 person | **Micro** (include generic) |
| 10-50 reviews | 1-2 people | 2-3 people | **Micro/Small** (include if confidence high) |
| 50-200 reviews | 2-3 people | 5-10 people | **Small** (personal only) |
| 200+ reviews | 3-8 people | 10+ people | **Small/Medium** (personal only) |

**Profession-Aware Thresholds**:
- **High-review professions** (doctors, lawyers): Solo practitioner can have 200+ reviews
- **Normal professions** (contractors, agencies): 200 reviews = 10+ people

**Decision Maker Validation**:
- If we found 3+ validated decision makers → OVERRIDE review estimate (definitely not micro)
- If we found 1-2 decision makers AND reviews suggest micro → HIGH CONFIDENCE micro
- If mismatch between signals → Use lower confidence, err on side of NOT including generic
